# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Families with children experiencing speech delays or hearing difficulties, and older adults navigating hearing loss. They arrive anxious, searching for reassurance that expert care exists close to home. Both groups share a need: a trusted, warm clinical partner who combines genuine expertise with human compassion. They're often comparing options and looking for signals of quality, modernity, and experience before making contact.

## Product Purpose

Hearwell's website converts worried visitors into confident patients. It communicates clinical excellence (16+ years experience, advanced diagnostics, personalized hearing aid fitting, evidence-led speech therapy) while making the first step easy: book an appointment or call. Two clinics in Kerala (Perumbavoor and Perinjanam) serve the local community. Success looks like a visitor who feels "these people know what they're doing, and they care about me" within the first 10 seconds.

## Brand Personality

**Modern, confident, premium.** Like a high-end medical practice that signals expertise through design.

Three words: *Precise. Warm. Authoritative.*

Voice: clear and direct, never clinical jargon for its own sake. Confident without being cold. Uses simple language that respects the reader's intelligence. Speaks as a trusted expert, not a salesperson.

## Anti-references

- **Generic hospital/clinic websites**: stock photo grids, blue-gradient hero banners, sterile card-heavy layouts with no personality. The "every clinic looks the same" problem.
- **SaaS/startup templates**: metric-theater dashboards, "supercharge your hearing" copy, tech-bro energy that doesn't belong in a healthcare context.
- **Cheap local business sites**: cluttered layouts, outdated aesthetics, Comic Sans energy, auto-playing videos, overwhelming CTAs.
- **AI-generic templates**: cream/sand backgrounds, identical card grids, numbered section eyebrows, gradient text, over-rounded cards. If it looks like "AI made this", it fails.

## Design Principles

1. **Expert confidence, not performance**: Let clinical competence show through precision in design. No need to shout credentials. The quality of the interface itself signals quality of care.
2. **Warmth through substance, not decoration**: Real patient stories, real doctor profiles, real clinic photos. Human warmth comes from authenticity, not ornament.
3. **Clarity is kindness**: Patients are anxious. Every element should reduce cognitive load, not add to it. Clear hierarchy, readable type, obvious next steps.
4. **Motion with purpose**: Animations should guide attention and create a sense of crafted care. Never decorative, never overwhelming. Respect that elderly patients and anxious parents need calm, not spectacle.
5. **One clinic, uncommonly crafted**: This is a local Kerala clinic, but the website should feel like it belongs to a practice that could be anywhere in the world. Premium without pretension.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA compliance minimum (4.5:1 contrast ratios for all text).
- Enhanced text readability for older users: body text ≥16px, generous line height, high-contrast text on all surfaces.
- Full keyboard navigation support.
- Screen reader compatible: semantic HTML, proper ARIA labels, alt text on all images.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations.
- Touch targets ≥44px for mobile (elderly users with reduced dexterity).
- No content gated behind animation (content visible by default, motion enhances).
